# cushion cut

*Cuts and shapes*

**A cushion cut is a square-to-rectangular diamond with rounded corners and gently curved sides, faceted in the brilliant style, so that it resembles a pillow. It offers soft, rounded sparkle and a slightly antique character.**

The cushion is a modified brilliant whose outline is a square or rectangle with the corners and sides rounded, giving it the pillow shape that names it. Brilliant-style faceting fills the interior, and cushions come in two broad faceting styles: a chunky pattern with larger, bolder facets, and a crushed-ice pattern with many smaller facets that scatter light into a glittery, granular sparkle.

Face-up, a cushion is softer and rounder than a princess and warmer in character than a round. It handles light well and is available in square and elongated versions, the latter flattering the finger the way any stretched shape does. Because the corners are rounded rather than pointed, a cushion has no fragile tips and sets securely, which makes it one of the more durable fancy shapes.

The honest practical note is that cushion covers a wide range of looks. A chunky cushion and a crushed-ice cushion can both be sold under the same name while looking quite different in the hand, one with distinct flashes and the other with a shimmering, less defined sparkle. Neither is better; it is a matter of taste. So it is worth asking which faceting style a given cushion uses, and viewing it face-up, rather than assuming all cushions sparkle alike. Length-to-width ratio also varies, from a true square near 1.0 to a clearly elongated rectangle, so the same name can describe very different proportions.
