# The Eye-Clean Buying Strategy (Prioritize Cut)

*Care and buying*

**The eye-clean strategy is a way to get the most beautiful diamond for your budget: put your money into cut, then pick a clarity grade that looks flawless to the naked eye rather than paying for perfection under magnification. Eye-clean means no inclusions are visible without magnification at a normal viewing distance.**

Of the 4Cs, cut has the biggest effect on how a diamond actually looks. Cut is how well the stone's facets are proportioned and finished, and it governs how much light comes back to your eye as brightness, fire, and sparkle. A poorly cut diamond looks dull no matter how good its other grades are, while an excellently cut stone looks lively. The round brilliant, with its 57 or 58 facets, was worked out to return light this way, and the proportions Marcel Tolkowsky published in 1919 remain a reference point for it. So the first move is to prioritize cut quality and not compromise there.

The second move is to stop paying for clarity you cannot see. Clarity grades describe inclusions viewed under 10x magnification, but at normal viewing distance many stones graded below flawless show nothing to the naked eye. A stone like that is called eye-clean. Rather than buying the highest clarity grade, you find the grade at which a particular diamond is eye-clean and stop there. Two stones with the same grade can differ, since inclusions can sit under a prong or off to the side, so you evaluate the individual diamond, not just the letter on the report.

The same logic applies to color: a near-colorless stone that faces up white gives you the look without paying for the top color grade. The result is a diamond that is brilliant and clean to the eye, with your budget spent where the eye can actually tell the difference. This works identically for Lab Grown Diamonds, which are graded on the same scales.

## Sources

- The modern round brilliant has 57 or 58 facets, and its light-returning proportions trace to Marcel Tolkowsky's published analysis in Diamond Design. (Source: Marcel Tolkowsky, Diamond Design, 1919)
- Lab Grown Diamonds are real diamonds graded on the same scales as mined diamonds. (Source: FTC Jewelry Guides, 2018)
