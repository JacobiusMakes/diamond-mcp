# hidden halo

*Settings and metals*

**A hidden halo is a ring of tiny diamonds set on the underside of the center stone's setting, circling the base where it meets the band, so the extra sparkle shows only from the side and profile, not when you look straight down at the ring.**

A hidden halo puts a halo where you do not expect one. Instead of framing the center diamond on top, the small stones ring the outside of the basket or collar that holds the center stone, sitting just below it. From directly above, the center diamond looks like a clean solitaire with no frame. Turn the ring to the side, though, and a band of light peeks out around the base, adding a hidden flash of sparkle and a bit of visual lift.

People choose a hidden halo when they want the crisp, uncluttered look of a solitaire from the top but still want the extra brilliance and the more detailed profile that a halo gives. It also adds sparkle without visually enlarging the outline of the center stone the way a traditional halo does, so the center shape stays pure. Because the accent stones sit low and close to the finger, they are fairly protected, though like any small pave-set stones they are held by tiny beads or prongs and benefit from an occasional check. A hidden halo does raise the center stone a little higher off the finger, which is worth knowing if you prefer a low profile or wear gloves often. It pairs with most center shapes, including round, oval, cushion, and elongated cuts, and it is a common way to dress up a design without changing how the center stone reads from above.
