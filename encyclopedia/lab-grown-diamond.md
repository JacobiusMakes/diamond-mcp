# Lab Grown Diamond

*Lab Grown Diamonds*

**A Lab Grown Diamond is a real diamond, pure crystallized carbon with the same crystal structure, hardness, and optical properties as a mined diamond, produced in a laboratory instead of forming underground. The only difference is where the carbon crystallized.**

A Lab Grown Diamond is not an imitation or a stand-in. It's the same material as a mined diamond: carbon atoms locked into the same cubic crystal lattice, with the same hardness (10 on the Mohs scale), the same brilliance, and the same fire. Set a grown and a mined diamond of matching grades side by side and no one, including a jeweler, can tell them apart by eye. Separating them takes specialized laboratory equipment, which is exactly what the grading labs use.

There are two ways to grow one, and each has its own entry. HPHT (High Pressure High Temperature) crystallizes carbon under conditions like those deep in the earth. CVD (Chemical Vapor Deposition) builds the crystal up layer by layer from a carbon-rich gas. Either method produces a genuine diamond; the choice is a matter of process, not of what the stone is.

The recognition is official. In 2018 the U.S. Federal Trade Commission revised its Jewelry Guides and removed the word "natural" from the definition of a diamond, acknowledging that a Lab Grown Diamond is a diamond (FTC Jewelry Guides, 2018). Independent labs grade them: IGI issues full 4C reports for Lab Grown Diamonds (IGI, 2026), while GIA moved Lab Grown Diamonds to descriptive Premium and Standard tiers on October 1, 2025 (GIA, 2025-10-01). An honest seller states plainly that a stone is a Lab Grown Diamond. That origin is a real fact about the stone, not a difference in what the stone is.
