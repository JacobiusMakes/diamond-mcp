# Lab-Grown vs Mined Diamonds

*Lab Grown Diamonds*

**A Lab Grown Diamond and a mined diamond are the same material, with the same hardness, brilliance, and fire; they differ in origin (a laboratory versus the earth) and usually in price, not in what they are.**

Start with what's identical, because most of it is. A Lab Grown Diamond and a mined diamond are both pure carbon in the same crystal structure. Both are a 10 on the Mohs scale, the hardest of any natural material. Both bend and return light the same way, so they show the same brilliance and fire. Both are graded by the same independent labs, and both are diamonds under the FTC's definition (FTC Jewelry Guides, 2018). The report format now differs a little by lab: IGI still issues full 4C reports for Lab Grown Diamonds (IGI, 2026), while GIA grades Lab Grown Diamonds on descriptive Premium and Standard tiers rather than individual 4C grades (GIA, 2025-10-01); mined diamonds still receive individual 4C grades from either lab. No one can tell a mined and a Lab Grown Diamond apart by eye; separating them takes laboratory instruments.

So what actually differs? Two things. The first is origin: one crystallized in the earth over a very long time, the other in a chamber over weeks. That's a real difference, and it's the one an honest seller discloses. Some people care about it, and that preference is legitimate. The second is price. A Lab Grown Diamond is usually less expensive than a mined diamond of similar size and quality, because producing one costs less, not because the stone is lesser.

A diamond, mined or grown, is not an investment. It's a love piece, something you choose and wear because it means something between two people, not an asset to hold for resale. Measuring either kind by what it might fetch later misreads the object. The sound way to choose is simple: pick the origin that means something to you, read the independent report behind the actual stone, and spend what fits your life.
