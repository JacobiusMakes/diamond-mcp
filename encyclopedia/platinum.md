# platinum

*Settings and metals*

**Platinum is a naturally white, dense, and durable precious metal used for fine jewelry, typically alloyed to about 90 to 95 percent purity. Unlike white gold, its white color is natural and permanent, so it never needs rhodium plating.**

Platinum is a premium setting metal prized for three things: color, density, and security. Its color is a true, cool white that comes from the metal itself, not from a coating, so a platinum ring stays white for life with no replating. Jewelry platinum is usually marked 950 or 900, meaning 95 or 90 percent pure platinum, a higher precious-metal content than even 18k gold. That purity also makes it a good option for people with metal sensitivities, since it contains little or no nickel.

Platinum is denser and heavier than gold, so a platinum ring has a noticeable, substantial weight on the hand. It is also excellent for holding diamonds: platinum prongs are strong and keep their grip well, which is why many jewelers favor it for securing valuable center stones and delicate pointed shapes. Platinum does scratch, but it wears differently from gold. Instead of losing metal, it mostly displaces, developing a soft matte surface called a patina over time. Some people love the patina; others prefer to have a jeweler polish the ring back to a high shine periodically. Either way, very little metal is actually lost. The trade-offs are cost and workability. Platinum is denser, so a ring uses more grams of metal, and the metal is harder to work, which is why platinum pieces usually cost more to make than comparable gold ones. It can also be a little more involved to resize, though a good jeweler resizes it routinely. For a white metal that never needs replating and holds stones securely, platinum is the durable, low-maintenance choice.
