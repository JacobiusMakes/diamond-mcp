# elongated cushion

*Cuts and shapes*

**An elongated cushion is a cushion cut with a rectangular rather than square outline, stretched so its length clearly exceeds its width while keeping the soft rounded corners and pillow silhouette. It is a longer, more finger-flattering version of the standard cushion.**

An elongated cushion is not a separate faceting family; it is a cushion cut, a modified brilliant with rounded corners and curved sides, drawn out to a longer length-to-width ratio. Where a classic cushion is close to square, an elongated cushion typically runs at a ratio of roughly 1.2 to one or more, giving it a rectangular pillow shape.

Face-up, that extra length does two useful things. It makes the stone read larger for its carat weight, because the outline spreads the same weight over a longer surface, and it lengthens and slims the look of the finger, the same reason people choose ovals and elongated radiants. The faceting can be the chunky style or the crushed-ice style, exactly as with a square cushion, so the sparkle character depends on the individual stone.

On durability the elongated cushion keeps the standard cushion's advantage: its corners are rounded, not pointed, so there is no fragile tip to chip, and it sits securely in a setting.

The practical point is that length is a preference, not a grade, and there is no single correct ratio. Some people want just a hint of stretch, others a long, narrow rectangle, and two stones of the same carat weight can look quite different depending on how far each is elongated. It is worth looking at the actual length-to-width ratio, and ideally the stone in person, since the number alone does not tell you how it will feel on the hand.
