# Clarity grade

*The 4Cs and grading*

**A clarity grade rates how free a diamond is of internal inclusions and surface blemishes when examined under 10x magnification, on a scale from Flawless down to Included. It describes tiny features inside and on the stone, most of which the naked eye can't see.**

The GIA scale, from best to most included, runs Flawless (FL), Internally Flawless (IF), Very Very Slightly Included (VVS1 and VVS2), Very Slightly Included (VS1 and VS2), Slightly Included (SI1 and SI2), and Included (I1, I2, I3). The reference condition is always 10x magnification: a Flawless diamond shows no inclusions or blemishes to a skilled grader at 10x, while an SI or I stone has features that are easier to find.

Internal features are called inclusions and surface features are called blemishes. They take many forms, such as tiny crystals, feathers, clouds, and needles, and a grade reflects their size, number, position, nature, and how much they affect the stone. Two diamonds with the same grade can look different, because an inclusion sitting under the table where you look straight in matters more than one tucked near the girdle under a prong.

For most buyers the practical target is a diamond that's clean to the unaided eye, which the trade calls eye-clean. Plenty of VS and even SI1 stones qualify, so a grade a step or two below Flawless often looks identical to the eye, with the difference living only under the loupe.

Lab Grown Diamonds are graded on the same clarity scale and assessed the same way under 10x. Their internal characteristics can differ in type from those in mined diamonds, but the grading language and the magnification standard are identical, and IGI still issues full 4C reports stating clarity.
