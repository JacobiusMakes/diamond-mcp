# Finding Your Ring Size

*Care and buying*

**Finding your ring size means measuring the finger the ring will sit on to get a size number. The most reliable way is to have a jeweler size you with a set of metal ring gauges, but you can also measure at home with a ring sizer or by measuring a ring that already fits.**

Ring size is a standardized measurement of the inside of a band. In the United States it is expressed as a number, with half sizes, that corresponds to a specific inside diameter and circumference. Getting it right matters, because a ring that is too loose can spin or slip off, and one that is too tight is uncomfortable and hard to remove.

The most accurate method is professional sizing. A jeweler slips a set of graduated metal rings, called a ring gauge, onto your finger until one fits, giving a true size on the finger you will actually wear the ring on. If you cannot get to a jeweler, there are good at-home options. Plastic or paper ring sizers work the same way as the gauge. You can also measure a ring that already fits that finger by measuring the inside diameter in millimeters across the center and matching it to a size chart. A strip of paper or string wrapped around the finger, marked, and measured against a chart works too, though it is the least precise.

A few things throw sizing off. Fingers swell over the day and in heat, so measure when your hands are warm and normal-sized, ideally later in the day rather than first thing in the morning or when you are cold. Account for the knuckle: the ring has to slide over it, so if your knuckle is larger than the base of the finger, size to clear the knuckle. Wide bands fit more snugly than thin ones, so size up slightly for a wide band. When in doubt, most rings can be resized later, though full eternity bands and certain settings are difficult or impossible to resize.
