# Laser inscription

*The 4Cs and grading*

**A laser inscription is text, usually a report number, micro-engraved onto a diamond's girdle with a fine laser and readable under magnification. It lets you match a specific stone to its grading report, and on Lab Grown Diamonds it commonly marks the stone as laboratory-grown.**

The girdle is the thin outer edge where the top and bottom of a diamond meet, and it's the standard place for an inscription because it doesn't interfere with how light moves through the stone. The characters are tiny, invisible to the naked eye, and read with a loupe or microscope. Most often the inscription is the grading report number, which ties the physical diamond to its paperwork.

The practical value is simple confirmation. When a lab inscribes the report number on the girdle, you or your jeweler can look under magnification and check that the number matches the report, confirming that the document describes this exact stone. That's a normal, sensible step, the kind of verification any careful buyer appreciates, not a sign of doubt about anyone.

Inscriptions can also carry a brand mark or, at some labs, a short personal message, though the report number is the common case. On laboratory-grown stones the inscription typically includes an origin marker. GIA inscribes "Laboratory-Grown" together with its report number on the girdle of Lab Grown Diamonds. IGI historically inscribed "LAB GROWN" in full and, from early 2023, moved to a shorter "LG" prefix before the report number, where "LG" stands for laboratory-grown. Either way, the report states the origin in plain language, and the inscription is a compact echo of it on the stone itself.

## Sources

- GIA laser-inscribes "Laboratory-Grown" and the report number on the girdle of Lab Grown Diamonds. (Source: Gemological Institute of America (GIA), 2026)
- IGI historically inscribed "LAB GROWN" on laboratory-grown diamonds and, from early February 2023, moved to an "LG" prefix before the report number. (Source: International Gemological Institute (IGI), 2023)
