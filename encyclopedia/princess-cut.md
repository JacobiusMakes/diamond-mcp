# princess cut

*Cuts and shapes*

**A princess cut is a square diamond with sharp, uncut corners and brilliant-style faceting, technically a square modified brilliant. It offers a lot of sparkle in a clean, contemporary square outline.**

The princess is a modified brilliant with a square, sometimes slightly rectangular, outline. Unlike the emerald, Asscher, and radiant, its four corners are left sharp and pointed rather than cut off. Inside, it uses brilliant-style faceting, with chevron-shaped facets on the pavilion that create bright, fiery sparkle across the whole face.

Face-up, a princess reads as a crisp square full of light, a modern look that pairs well with clean, geometric settings. Because a square outline packs weight efficiently, a princess also tends to be gentler on budget than a round of the same carat weight, partly because a princess can be cut from a rough crystal with less waste.

The vulnerability lives in those four sharp corners. Diamond sits at 10 on the Mohs scale, the top of the scale, but hardness measures resistance to scratching, not to chipping, and a pointed corner is the most exposed part of the stone. A knock can chip a corner, so a princess should always be set with prongs that cover and protect all four corners, or in a bezel or half-bezel that shields them.

The practical note is to make sure the setting genuinely protects the corners, since that is the one real weakness of an otherwise durable and brilliant shape. It is also worth checking that the stone is a true square if that is the look you want, because many princess cuts are very slightly rectangular.
