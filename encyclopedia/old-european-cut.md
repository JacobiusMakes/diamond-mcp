# old european cut

*Cuts and shapes*

**An old European cut is an antique round brilliant: a circular, hand-cut diamond with a high crown, a small table, a deep pavilion, and a large flat culet. It is the direct ancestor of the modern round brilliant.**

The old European cut is the round member of the antique brilliant family and the closest predecessor to today's round brilliant. Like the modern round it has a circular outline and roughly the same count of brilliant-style facets, but its proportions come from the hand-cutting era: a tall crown, a small table, a steep and deep pavilion, and a large culet that shows face-up as a small open circle at the very center of the stone.

Face-up, an old European cut looks warmer and chunkier than a modern round. It was cut for candlelight and soft indoor light rather than bright modern lighting, so it returns light in broad, bold flashes with more visible fire and less of the small, uniform sparkle a modern round is engineered to produce. That distinct older character is exactly why many people seek these stones out.

Its round outline has no points or corners, so like a modern round it is a durable, secure shape to set.

The practical note is that old European cuts predate standardized, machine-assisted cutting, so their symmetry is often slightly irregular and each stone is individual. They are best appreciated on their own terms rather than judged against modern ideal-cut light-return standards, which they were never cut to meet. As with old mine cuts, new stones are sometimes cut today in the old European style, so ask whether a given stone is a genuine antique or a modern interpretation if that distinction matters to you.
