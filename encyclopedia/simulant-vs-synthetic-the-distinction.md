# Simulant vs synthetic (the distinction)

*Materials and simulants*

**A synthetic diamond (a Lab Grown Diamond) is the same material as a mined diamond, grown in a lab instead of forming in the Earth. A simulant is a different material that merely looks like diamond. In short: a Lab Grown Diamond is a real diamond, a simulant is not.**

This is the single most useful distinction in the whole subject, and it is often blurred. Synthetic and simulant are not synonyms.

A synthetic diamond, better called a Lab Grown Diamond, has the identical chemistry (pure carbon), crystal structure, hardness, and optical properties of a mined diamond. It is grown by high-pressure high-temperature (HPHT) or chemical vapor deposition (CVD) methods instead of forming in the Earth, but it is diamond in every physical sense. U.S. regulators make this official. The FTC's 2018 Jewelry Guides define a diamond by its composition and dropped the word natural, so a Lab Grown Diamond is a diamond and must be disclosed as such, never passed off as mined.

A simulant, also called an imitation, is a different material chosen because it resembles diamond to the eye. Cubic zirconia, moissanite, white sapphire, YAG (yttrium aluminum garnet), and even glass are all diamond simulants. They can look similar, but they differ in hardness, density, heat and electrical behavior, and fire, which is exactly how they are identified.

The practical takeaway is simple. Lab Grown Diamond and simulant describe completely different things. A Lab Grown Diamond is a diamond that happens to be grown rather than mined. A simulant is not a diamond at all, whatever it looks like. Good sellers state plainly which one a stone is, and the terms above are the words to look for.

## Sources

- The FTC's revised Jewelry Guides define a diamond by composition and require Lab Grown Diamonds to be clearly disclosed as such, while simulants (imitations) must be disclosed as not being diamonds. (Source: FTC, Guides for the Jewelry, Precious Metals, and Pewter Industries (Federal Register), 2018-08-16)
