# Certification labs

*The 4Cs and grading*

**Certification labs, more precisely gemological grading laboratories, are independent organizations that examine and grade diamonds to published standards. For Lab Grown Diamonds today the main names are GIA, IGI, and GCAL, and the landscape shifted noticeably in 2025 and 2026.**

GIA, the Gemological Institute of America, is the nonprofit founded by Robert M. Shipley in 1931 that built the 4Cs framework the whole trade uses. It grades both mined and laboratory-grown stones. On October 1, 2025, GIA moved its Lab Grown Diamond reports to a Premium and Standard tier structure, a change from the letter-and-number 4C grades it long issued for them.

IGI, the International Gemological Institute, was founded in Antwerp in 1975 and is the largest grading laboratory by volume of reports. It's the dominant lab for Lab Grown Diamonds, and it still issues full 4C reports for them, stating color, clarity, cut, and carat the traditional way. For that reason most laboratory-grown stones on the market today carry an IGI report.

GCAL, the Gem Certification and Assurance Lab, was founded in New York in 2001 and is known for guaranteed grading and its 8X cut grade for round brilliants, introduced in 2021. It grades laboratory-grown as well as mined diamonds and is a respected independent option, particularly for buyers who value its cut analysis.

One more shift marks the current landscape: HRD Antwerp, a long-standing European lab, stopped grading loose Lab Grown Diamonds in 2026. The practical takeaway is straightforward. Match the diamond to its report by the laser-inscribed number, read the grades on their own terms, and remember that a laboratory-grown diamond is a real diamond that these same laboratories grade by the same science. Which lab graded a stone tells you the standard behind the paperwork, which is simply useful to know.
