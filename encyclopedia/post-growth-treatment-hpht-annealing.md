# Post-Growth Treatment (HPHT Annealing)

*Lab Grown Diamonds*

**Post-growth treatment is a process applied to a Lab Grown Diamond after it's grown to improve its color, most often HPHT annealing, in which the finished crystal is exposed to high pressure and high temperature that shift its color toward colorless. The change is permanent and is disclosed on the grading report.**

Some Lab Grown Diamonds, particularly those made by CVD, come out of growth with a faint brown or gray cast. The color comes from the way atoms and tiny gaps are arranged in the crystal as it forms. Post-growth treatment corrects this. The most common form is HPHT annealing: the finished diamond is placed under high pressure and high temperature, the same kind of environment used to grow HPHT diamonds, which rearranges those atomic-scale features and clears the tint, moving the stone toward colorless.

Two things are worth understanding plainly. First, the change is permanent and stable. HPHT annealing alters the crystal itself, not a coating on the surface, so the improved color doesn't wear off, wash away, or revert under normal conditions. Second, it doesn't make the stone any less of a diamond. It's still pure crystallized carbon with the same hardness; the treatment only adjusts color.

Treatment is a normal, well-understood part of diamond production, for grown and mined stones alike, and the standard is transparency. Independent grading reports note when a diamond has been treated, so the information travels with the stone. Checking the report to see how a diamond was grown and whether it was treated is simply good practice, the same way you'd read the details on anything you're buying. It's a way to know exactly what you have, not a reason for worry.
