# Annual Prong Check

*Care and buying*

**An annual prong check is a yearly appointment where a jeweler inspects the small metal claws holding your diamond and re-tightens or rebuilds any that have worn down. It is the single best habit for not losing a stone.**

Prongs are the small metal tips that curl over the edge of a diamond to hold it in place. They take the brunt of daily life: every time a ring bumps a doorknob, a countertop, or a car door, the prongs absorb it. Over months and years that contact wears the metal thin, and a thin prong can bend, snag, or break. When a prong fails, the stone can loosen and fall out, which is why worn prongs are worth taking seriously. The risk is almost entirely preventable with a regular check.

The fix is a quick inspection about once a year. A jeweler looks at each prong under magnification, checks that the tips still sit firmly over the stone, and feels whether the diamond has any play in the setting. If a prong is worn, they can re-tip it by adding a small amount of metal to the end, or, if several are worn, rebuild the head. You can sometimes catch a warning sign yourself: if you tap the ring gently near your ear and hear a faint rattle, or if the stone visibly shifts, have it looked at sooner.

Many jewelers do this inspection for free, and some jewelry insurance policies expect regular maintenance as a condition of coverage. Rings worn every day, and rings with tall or delicate settings, benefit from a check more often than once a year. Pairing the prong check with a professional cleaning turns it into a single easy annual habit that keeps the stone secure and the ring bright.
