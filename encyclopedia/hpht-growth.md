# HPHT Growth

*Lab Grown Diamonds*

**HPHT (High Pressure High Temperature) is a method of growing diamond by dissolving carbon in molten metal and letting it crystallize onto a diamond seed under intense pressure and heat, recreating the kind of conditions under which diamonds form deep in the earth.**

HPHT recreates, inside a machine, the pressure and heat that form diamonds underground. A large press squeezes a sealed growth cell to enormous pressure while heating it to a high temperature. Inside sits a carbon source, usually graphite, along with a molten metal that acts as a solvent, commonly iron, nickel, or cobalt. The heat dissolves the carbon into the liquid metal, and the dissolved carbon travels through the melt to a cooler zone where a small diamond seed is placed. There the carbon crystallizes onto the seed, and a diamond grows.

The result is a real diamond, chemically and physically identical to a mined one. HPHT-grown crystals often take a distinctive shape with both cube and octahedron faces, and they frequently contain a little nitrogen, which can lend a faint yellow tone. Growers can adjust the recipe to control that.

HPHT does double duty in the diamond world. Besides growing diamonds from scratch, the same high-pressure, high-temperature environment is used as a post-growth treatment to improve the color of already-grown stones, and to treat some mined diamonds as well. When a Lab Grown Diamond has been color-treated this way, a reputable grading report says so.

The two growth methods, HPHT and CVD, reach the same result by different means. HPHT builds the crystal in a metal melt under earth-like pressure; CVD assembles it from gas at low pressure. Both produce genuine diamonds, graded on the same terms.
