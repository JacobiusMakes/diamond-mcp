# Do Lab Grown Diamonds Cloud or Fade?

*Lab Grown Diamonds*

**No. Lab Grown Diamonds do not cloud, fade, yellow, or degrade over time; a Lab Grown Diamond is crystallized carbon, and its color and clarity are fixed properties of the crystal that don't change with age or wear.**

This is one of the most common worries about Lab Grown Diamonds, and the answer is a plain no. A diamond, grown or mined, is carbon locked in an extremely stable crystal structure and rated 10 on the Mohs scale, the hardest of any natural material. Its color and clarity are set by the arrangement of the crystal itself, not by any coating or dye, so they don't drift over time. Sunlight, water, heat from daily life, and normal wear don't cloud it, fade it, or turn it yellow.

The worry usually comes from mixing up Lab Grown Diamonds with diamond simulants. Cubic zirconia, a different and softer material, can abrade and grow cloudy after years of wear. That's a real thing that happens, but it happens to cubic zirconia, not to diamond. Because a Lab Grown Diamond is a real diamond, it behaves like one, staying clear and bright.

Color treatment doesn't change this either. When a Lab Grown Diamond has had its color improved by HPHT annealing, that change is permanent and stable, so a treated stone doesn't revert under everyday conditions.

There is one ordinary exception, and it isn't the stone changing. Any diamond can look dull when a film of skin oil, soap, or lotion builds up on it. That's surface grime sitting on top of the diamond, not the diamond degrading, and a quick clean with mild soap and water brings the sparkle back. The crystal underneath is exactly as it was the day it was set.
