# Made-to-Order vs. Ready-to-Ship

*Care and buying*

**Made-to-order means your ring is built and set after you place the order, so you can choose the stone, setting, metal, and size, but it takes longer. Ready-to-ship means the ring is already finished and in stock, so it arrives fast with less to customize.**

These are two ways a finished ring reaches you, and the difference is mostly time versus choice.

A made-to-order ring, sometimes called built-to-order, is assembled after you buy it. You typically select the center stone, the setting style, the metal, and the finger size, and the piece is set and finished to those specifications. Because a person is mounting the stone and completing the ring for your order, it takes longer, often a couple of weeks or more depending on the maker. The payoff is control: you get the exact combination you want, and you can often choose the specific center diamond by its grading report.

A ready-to-ship ring is already made and sitting in inventory in a set configuration and size. It ships quickly, sometimes within a day or two, which matters if you have a deadline like a planned proposal. The trade-off is less flexibility. You take the stone, setting, and size as offered, though many rings can still be resized afterward.

Which one fits depends on your priorities. If you know exactly what you want, care about picking the individual stone, or want a less common combination, made-to-order is the way, and you should ask for the realistic lead time before you commit. If you are on a tight timeline or you have found a ready-made ring you love, ready-to-ship removes the wait. Either way, ask what is included, how sizing is handled, and what the return or exchange window is, since a made-to-order piece sometimes carries a different return policy than a stock one.
