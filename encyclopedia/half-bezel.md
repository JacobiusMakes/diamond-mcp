# half bezel

*Settings and metals*

**A half bezel, also called a semi-bezel or open bezel, wraps metal around only two sides of the diamond, usually the left and right, leaving the other two sides open. It shields the stone's long sides while letting in more light than a full bezel.**

A half bezel is the middle ground between a full bezel and prongs. Two curved walls of metal cup the sides of the stone and hold it firmly, while the top and bottom, or the two ends of an elongated stone, stay open. That open space lets light enter from more directions, so a half-bezeled diamond usually looks brighter and a little larger than one in a full bezel, while the metal on the sides still braces a long stretch of the girdle.

This setting works especially well for elongated shapes, whether worn lengthwise or east-west, because the metal can brace the long sides while leaving the ends open to show the stone's full length. It is a practical pick for someone who wants more protection and less upkeep than prongs give, without fully framing the stone in metal. As with a full bezel, there are no prong tips to catch or wear, which makes a half bezel low-fuss for daily wear. The open edges do leave the uncovered corners or ends more exposed, so for a pointed shape a jeweler will often combine a half bezel on the sides with a V-tip guarding each point. Half bezels can be finished plain, with milgrain, or with a thin bright-cut edge, and they pair naturally with clean, modern ring designs. When you compare it to a full bezel, you are trading a bit of edge coverage for more light and a slightly larger look.
